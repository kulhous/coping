# Image Generation Guide — Coping Skills Cards

## Visual Style

**Line art, hand-drawn illustration style** — organic, slightly imperfect ink strokes with varying line weight (thicker for outlines, thinner for detail). The look is warm and human, like a skilled sketch in a therapist's notebook. Not clinical, not cartoonish — somewhere between naive art and editorial illustration.

### Key Characteristics

- **Transparent background** — no fills, no frames
- **Palette:** Black linework + white (negative space / highlights) + optionally **one muted pastel accent** (e.g., dusty rose, soft sage, warm apricot, lavender) used sparingly — only for a focal element or gentle area fill, never dominant
- **Lines:** Flowing, rounded, organic — no sharp angles, no jagged edges, no straight mechanical lines. Strokes taper naturally as if drawn with a felt-tip pen or brush pen
- **Shapes:** Soft, curved, enclosed. Circles and rounded forms dominate. Conveys safety and containment
- **Composition:** Centered, icon-like, readable at 80×80 px thumbnail size. Single focal motif, no background scene
- **Mood:** Calm, grounding, gently empowering. Never depicts distress, pain, isolation, or darkness. Every image communicates agency and self-care
- **Recurring visual language:** Open hands (giving, holding, protecting), gentle organic curves, dot-and-line connection motifs (like molecules or constellations), nature elements simplified to soft abstractions

## Psychological Design Principles (for at-risk youth 8–18)

1. **No depictions of the problem** — show the *solution*, never the distress. A breathing card shows lungs expanding gently, not a panicking figure
2. **Rounded, enclosed forms** — convey psychological safety and containment. Avoid sharp, fractured, or scattered compositions
3. **Hands as agency** — when hands appear, they are open, active, holding, creating — never clenched, reaching desperately, or empty
4. **Abstract over literal** — a metaphor (wave, cocoon, gentle spiral) is safer and more inclusive than a literal depiction of a crying child
5. **No faces showing distress** — if a face/figure appears, it should be neutral or gently positive. Prefer faceless/abstract figures
6. **Warmth without intensity** — the single pastel accent should feel like a warm blanket, not an alarm. Dusty, muted tones only
7. **No triggers** — absolutely no sharp objects, red marks on skin, tears, darkness/shadows, isolation imagery, or anything that could normalize or romanticize self-harm

## Technical Spec

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Background | Transparent (PNG) |
| Colors | Black + white + max 1 muted pastel (optional per card) |
| Pastel usage | ≤ 20% of drawn area; omit entirely if the icon reads clearly without it |
| Line style | Hand-drawn brush pen, varying weight, organic/rounded |
| Canvas | Square, icon-friendly (recommend 512×512 generation, display at 80–120 px) |
| Content safety | No distress, no sharp objects, no isolation, no faces in pain |
| Tone | Calm, warm, grounding, empowering |

## Example Prompts

### Sour Candy (sensory grounding — taste)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. A single open hand holding a small round candy, with 2–3 tiny radiating curved lines around the candy suggesting intensity. One soft apricot fill on the candy only. Organic brush-pen strokes, rounded forms, calm and inviting.

### Butterfly Hug (bilateral stimulation — self-soothing)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. Two crossed hands resting gently on opposite shoulders, seen from the front — simplified, no face. Small symmetrical curved lines on each side suggesting gentle tapping motion, like butterfly wings. Soft lavender accent on the wing-like motion lines only. Warm, safe, contained composition.

### Box Breath (breathing regulation)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. A softly rounded square shape made of a single flowing line, with a small gentle spiral or breeze-like curve inside suggesting breath. Black ink with varying stroke weight. Optional: one dusty sage accent on the spiral. Minimal, meditative, grounding.

### Brain Dump (journaling — cognitive offloading)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. An open notebook or paper with a few loose, playful scribble lines flowing upward and outward from the page, as if thoughts are being released. Lines organic and free. Soft dusty rose accent on one or two of the floating scribble lines. Feels like relief, not chaos.

### Shake It Out (somatic release — movement)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. A simple, faceless standing figure with arms slightly out, surrounded by small curved motion lines radiating outward from the body suggesting gentle shaking/vibration. Black linework only — no pastel needed. Energetic but safe, like shaking off rain.

### Safe Place (guided imagery — visualization)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. A soft, cocoon-like rounded enclosure (like a nest or gentle cave shape) with a tiny abstract figure curled peacefully inside. One warm apricot fill on the enclosure walls. Conveys protection, warmth, chosen solitude (not isolation).

### Cold Splash (sensory interrupt — temperature)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. Two cupped hands with a few droplet shapes above them and small curved lines suggesting coolness. Soft pale blue accent on 2–3 droplets only. Gentle, refreshing, not shocking.

### Rip & Tear (physical release — safe destruction)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. Two hands pulling apart a sheet of paper with a few small pieces floating outward. Lines are dynamic but still rounded and organic — conveys healthy release, not aggression. Black ink only, no pastel. Movement feels satisfying, not violent.

### Cloud Watch (mindful observation — nature)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. Two or three soft, rounded cloud shapes with a single upward-gazing eye or a simple upturned face beneath them (abstract, no detailed features). One soft sage accent on the largest cloud. Peaceful, expansive, contemplative.

### Humming (vagal toning — sound)

> Hand-drawn line art icon, transparent background. A simplified side-profile of a face with closed eyes and slightly parted lips, with small concentric curved lines emanating outward from the mouth/throat area suggesting gentle vibration. Soft lavender on one or two of the sound curves. Serene and self-contained.
